Why Old School? Because many renovations by modern "experts" do renovations that are merely cosmetic, and don't seem to replicate the original strength and beauty of a home. Doing things the "old School way" involves doing an upgrade or renovation without losing the character or "intrinsic value" (the reason you bought it) of the home. Here in the prairies we have many homes that are classic, or even century homes. They require a special kind of care and a special touch as "back in the day" things were built to last, and art work and craftmanship was paramount.

Although it's a specialty, work is not restricted to older homes. Any home where the TLC touch is required should have a craftsman at the helm of any repairs or renovations.
